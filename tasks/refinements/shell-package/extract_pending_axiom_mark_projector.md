## Why it needs to be done

The deferred-task registration in [`extract_cytoscape_projectors.md`](extract_cytoscape_projectors.md) Decision §3 named one firing condition (second + third caller materializes) and noted that the WBS closer would register this task with effort + one-line description so the orchestrator pick-task pass would surface it on cadence. The pick-task pass did surface it. The orchestrator pass cannot itself verify whether the named preconditions are actually met at refinement time — that is a refinement-stage activity. This audit is that activity.

Three observations make the audit valuable (rather than a no-op formality):

1. **The deferral note's framing was "the three-caller policy applies when the participant and audience land pending-axiom-mark rendering" — i.e., the preconditions were stated as a hypothetical "when" rather than a "soon."** At code-read time, the WBS leaves that would materialize the second and third callers are not just unscheduled — both predecessor refinements ([`part_axiom_mark_decoration.md`](../participant-ui/part_axiom_mark_decoration.md) and [`aud_axiom_mark_decoration.md`](../audience/aud_axiom_mark_decoration.md)) **explicitly decided to NOT render pending-axiom-marks** for principled reasons (the at-a-glance signal at both broadcast surfaces is "ratified bedrock," not in-flight proposals). The pending variant is moderator-only by methodology choice, not by oversight. Recording this in an audit prevents a future implementer (or orchestrator pass) from picking the lift back up under the mistaken assumption that the second and third callers are merely "not yet built" rather than "principledly absent at present."

2. **The committed-axiom-mark sibling `extract_axiom_marks` shipped under the three-caller policy at the same cadence; the pending variant is structurally distinct.** The committed projector lifted because moderator + participant + audience each independently needed to paint a per-node committed-mark badge as their at-a-glance "ratified bedrock" affordance — three callers with identical projection shape. The pending projector serves a different concern: the moderator paints dashed-faded "proposal-in-flight" dots as a proposal-author affordance ([`PendingAxiomMarkBadge.tsx:49-52`](../../../apps/moderator/src/graph/PendingAxiomMarkBadge.tsx#L49) per [`mod_axiom_mark_pending_render.md`](../moderator-ui/mod_axiom_mark_pending_render.md) Decision §4). Participants and audience members are not the proposal author at the broadcast surface; the moderator is. The asymmetry is a real methodology asymmetry, not a planning lag. The audit records it.

3. **Closing this leaf cleanly prevents pick-task queue churn.** Leaving the `extract_pending_axiom_mark_projector` leaf open on the orchestrator's pick-task queue without an audit means every orchestrator pass picks it up and re-refines it; refinement-document churn produces nothing new. The closer marks this leaf `complete 100`; the future-trigger work is held by a named-future-task (Decision §4) and does not block the queue.

The follow-on benefit of the audit:

- **Prevents premature ossification.** The three-caller policy's whole point is to wait until empirical convergence rather than to design-shape-and-hope. Lifting `projectPendingAxiomMarks` into the shell today (as a single-caller move "in case" the second and third callers eventually materialize) would create a shell-side projector whose only consumer is the moderator's `GraphCanvasPane` — exactly the "single-caller shell entry with one workspace user" anti-pattern that `extract_cytoscape_projectors` Decision §1 rejected for the participant boolean+count helpers, and that `extract_facet_pill` Decision §2 set the three-caller policy to prevent.
- **Records the named-future-task path crisply.** The orchestrator brief's tech-debt registration policy asks every deferred task to be named with effort + description; Decision §4 supplies both.
- **Pins the recipe in advance.** When the trigger does fire (likely 1-3 quarters out, after the participant's pending-proposals pane and the audience's broadcast-feedback iteration both surface a need), the implementer of `_v2` reads this audit and follows the `extract_cytoscape_projectors` / `shell_axiom_marks_extraction` recipe verbatim — no design re-derivation needed.

## Decisions

### §1 — The trigger is unmet AND the second + third callers are principledly absent (not merely unscheduled); defer

The deferral note in [`extract_cytoscape_projectors.md` Decision §3 line 241](extract_cytoscape_projectors.md) registered this task with the trigger *"once a second + third caller materialize (likely participant + audience pending-axiom-mark rendering)."* The audit at refinement time finds:

- **The participant surface explicitly excludes pending-axiom-mark rendering.** [`part_axiom_mark_decoration.md:27`](../participant-ui/part_axiom_mark_decoration.md#L27): *"The participant equivalent is not in this leaf — the participant's at-a-glance signal is 'ratified bedrock'; pending axiom-marks are an in-flight proposal that lives under the (future) `part_pending_proposals.*` group when the participant gets a pending-proposals pane."* No `part_pending_proposals.*` group exists in the WBS today.
- **The audience surface explicitly excludes pending-axiom-mark rendering.** [`aud_axiom_mark_decoration.md:38`](../audience/aud_axiom_mark_decoration.md#L38): *"The audience equivalent is NOT in this leaf — the broadcast surface's at-a-glance signal is 'ratified bedrock' … A future polish leaf (`aud_axiom_mark_pending_render`, ~1d, named-future-task — closer registers in WBS if/when the broadcast feedback identifies pending-axiom-mark visualization as load-bearing) can land it. NOT pre-registered today (speculative …)."*

Both exclusions are settled refinements (`complete 100` on their respective `.tji` leaves) with explicit prose rationale. The deferral is therefore not a planning lag — it is a principled methodology choice: pending-axiom-marks are an in-flight-proposal affordance that the proposal-authoring surface (moderator) renders, and the at-a-glance broadcast surfaces (participant, audience) deliberately do not.

Three options for handling the divergence:

- **(A — chosen)** Treat the second + third callers as principledly absent at this commit; defer the lift; register a single shell-package follow-up named-future-task (`shell_package.extract_pending_axiom_mark_projector_v2`, Decision §4) whose trigger condition is *"once a participant pending-axiom-mark rendering leaf AND an audience pending-axiom-mark rendering leaf both land."* The shell-extract lift fires only after both UI leaves materialize and the three-caller convergence check passes. Reasoning: this honors the three-caller policy (`extract_facet_pill` Decision §2) mechanically AND respects the participant + audience surfaces' explicit committed-only decisions. The future `_v2` task's trigger reads the actual three-caller-convergence condition; it does not lift on a calendar date.
- **(B)** Lift the four symbols into `packages/shell/src/pending-axiom-marks/` now, with one workspace caller (moderator), under the rationale that the projector's coupling to ADR 0030 §9 makes it "shell-shaped." Cost: introduces a single-caller shell entry with one workspace user — the anti-pattern that `extract_cytoscape_projectors` Decision §1 rejected for the participant boolean+count helpers, and that `extract_facet_pill` Decision §2 set the three-caller policy to prevent. The moderator's single caller is not a convergence event; the four symbols would sit in the shell as moderator-only-by-import-path, increasing surface area without reducing duplication. Rejected.
- **(C)** Merge the pending variant into the existing `packages/shell/src/axiom-marks/` namespace (a single shell entry hosting `AxiomMark` + `PendingAxiomMark` + both projectors). Cost: blurs the per-vocabulary directory naming the shell substrate established (`packages/shell/src/<vocabulary>/`); the committed and pending variants have different terminator semantics, different proposal-arrival vs commit-time ordering rules ([`mod_axiom_mark_pending_render.md`](../moderator-ui/mod_axiom_mark_pending_render.md) Decision §1 vs `projectAxiomMarks`'s commit-time ordering), and different downstream-consumer shapes (`<PendingAxiomMarkBadge>` paints dashed-faded; `<AxiomMarkBadge>` paints solid). Conflating them under one namespace would force the future second + third caller authors to discover which symbols carry which semantics by reading source; the sibling-directory split makes the vocabulary boundary visible. Rejected.

Chosen: (A). The three-caller policy floor is three; the current caller count is one. The natural path is to wait until the floor is reached, then lift via the same recipe `extract_cytoscape_projectors` and `shell_axiom_marks_extraction` followed.
