## Why it needs to be done

Three near-identical copies of the same ~400-line module (`diagnosticHighlights.ts`) and three near-identical `WsSessionState extends BaseWsSessionState` overrides + `applyDiagnostic` re-implementations live in three workspaces today. The duplication is structurally byte-identical for the shared symbols (the audience drops `projectDiagnosticHighlights` because it never paints per-entity steady-state highlights and adds the two `flatten*` sibling helpers its fire overlays consume; the participant adds the `nodeHasDiagnostic` / `edgeHasDiagnostic` / `diagnosticSeverityFor` thin helpers; otherwise the moderator's original is the verbatim reference both other surfaces mirror). The cost of leaving the duplication in place is the standard cross-surface drift risk plus a **load-bearing** drift risk for this specific vocabulary: any change to the server's `identityKeyFor` formula MUST be mirrored in all three client copies or the `fired` / `cleared` matching at the WS-store layer silently breaks (a `cleared` whose key doesn't match the previously-fired key leaks an "active" entry in the store forever). A single drift between the moderator's mirror and the participant's mirror, or between either of those and the audience's mirror, would cause the affected surface's diagnostic-overlay state to skew from the others; the resulting bug would surface only at the seam where the affected surface renders.

The three predecessor refinements documented this exact risk verbatim (see [`aud_diagnostic_fire_animation.md`](../audience/aud_diagnostic_fire_animation.md) "Parallel client mirror" docblock at [`apps/audience/src/graph/diagnosticHighlights.ts:32-40`](../../../apps/audience/src/graph/diagnosticHighlights.ts#L32) — "All three callers MUST stay in lockstep on the identity-key formula or the `fired` / `cleared` matching at the WS store layer breaks") and pre-registered this leaf as the architectural fix.

The third-caller policy (from [`extract_facet_pill.md`](extract_facet_pill.md) Decision §2) exists for this case: at two callers the API shape is still under negotiation (the audience's port adds `flattenActiveDiagnosticsForFire` + `flattenActiveDiagnosticsForEdgeFire` as new helpers the moderator + participant don't carry — that addition would have ossified prematurely if the shell extraction had fired at two callers); at three callers the convergence point is empirical. The audience's `aud_diagnostic_fire_animation` shipped 2026-05-29 with a verbatim port of the moderator+participant identity-key formula AND with the two sibling fire-tuple helpers; the audience's `aud_diagnostic_edge_fire_animation` shipped the same day with the edge-tuple addendum. Three callers; the API surface is empirically converged; the lift can lock in the union of the three.

The WS-store slot lift is a tightly-coupled follow-on. With the helpers in the shell, the three per-app `wsStore.ts` files carry an `activeDiagnostics` override that depends on the shell-resident `diagnosticIdentityKey` — they collapse cleanly back to a one-line re-export of `createDefaultWsStore()` (audience) or a thin `withDevtools(...)`-wrapped initializer (moderator + participant). Without the slot lift, the duplication persists at the store layer even after the helper layer consolidates, defeating half the point.

The follow-on benefits:

- **One source of truth for diagnostic identity + projection.** A future ADR or refinement that touches diagnostic semantics (e.g. adding a new diagnostic kind, refining the rollup rules, changing the identity-key formula in lockstep with a server-side change) edits one `packages/shell/src/diagnostics/` block + one shell-side test suite, instead of three workspaces + three suites. The next surface to land diagnostic rendering (replay-test, OBS composite, any future surface) imports from `@a-conversa/shell` directly — no cross-workspace ports, no fourth-caller-extraction registration.
- **Identity-key drift becomes structurally impossible across surfaces.** The lockstep-mirror risk that all three predecessor refinements documented is dissolved at the same moment.
- **WS-store shape consolidation.** The three near-identical `WsSessionState extends BaseWsSessionState` widenings collapse; the `activeDiagnostics` field becomes canonical on `BaseWsSessionState` itself; future surfaces inherit it from the shell's default factory.
- **Test consolidation.** Three Vitest suites (~390 / 355 / 328 lines) covering the same identity formula + the same affected-entities projection + the same rollup rules + the same flatten-helpers collapse into one. Cross-surface contract regressions become structurally impossible because there is only one implementation to test against.

This leaf is registered as the closer's follow-up to `audience.aud_animations.aud_diagnostic_fire_animation` (2026-05-29) per the orchestrator brief's tech-debt registration policy; the source-of-debt refinement explicitly named both halves of the lift (helpers + store slot).

## Decisions

### §1 — `packages/shell/src/diagnostics/` sibling directory (not folded into `ws/` or `axiom-marks/`)

The lifted vocabulary touches two existing shell areas: the WS store (the `activeDiagnostics` slot canonicalization on `BaseWsSessionState`) and the entity-layer disposition family (axiom marks already lift here under `packages/shell/src/axiom-marks/`). Three options for the canonical home of the projection helpers + wire types + flatten helpers:

- **(A — chosen)** A new sibling directory `packages/shell/src/diagnostics/` with the single `diagnostic-highlights.ts` module + its barrel + its test. The shell's `defaultStore.ts` imports `diagnosticIdentityKey` from `../diagnostics/diagnostic-highlights.js` (intra-package relative — the orthodox pattern inside `packages/shell/src/**`).
- **(B)** Fold the helpers into `packages/shell/src/ws/` because the WS store consumes the identity-key formula. Cost: `ws/` is currently scoped to the WS-client / WS-store substrate (the connection lifecycle, the store contract, the default factory, the client provider) — the projection helpers + wire types are entity-layer projection vocabulary that happen to be consumed by the store, not store internals. Folding them in would blur the `ws/` directory's role from "wire-substrate primitives" to "wire-substrate primitives + diagnostic projection vocabulary," making the seam less legible.
- **(C)** Fold the helpers into `packages/shell/src/axiom-marks/` (rename it `entity-decorations/` or similar) because both are entity-layer disposition vocabularies. Cost: a directory rename ripples through every existing axiom-marks consumer; the two vocabularies serve different layers (axiom-marks are per-participant *declarations* on a node; diagnostics are *methodology-engine output* on the graph structure) and the shell's prior extractions established the "one vocabulary per directory" convention (every `packages/shell/src/<area>/` carries a single conceptual surface). A cross-vocabulary directory rename would break the convention for a marginal organisational benefit.

Chosen: (A). The sibling-directory layout matches the precedent every prior shell extraction established (`auth/`, `facet-pill/`, `axiom-marks/`, `annotations/`, `slots/`, `votes-by-facet/`, `facet-status/`); puts the entity-disposition vocabulary side-by-side with the existing `axiom-marks/` sibling per ADR 0027's layer-separation principle; keeps the `ws/` directory focused on wire-substrate primitives.

The relative intra-package import (`../diagnostics/diagnostic-highlights.js`) inside `defaultStore.ts` is the orthodox pattern inside `packages/shell/src/**`; importing from `@a-conversa/shell` inside the shell itself would introduce a self-circular dependency. Mirrors the precedent set in `shell_axiom_marks_extraction` Decision §2 (the lifted `AxiomMarkBadge.tsx` imports `axiomMarkColorFor` from `../facet-pill/participant-color.js`).

### §2 — Single-module `diagnostic-highlights.ts` (not split into `projection.ts` + `fire-tuples.ts` + `wire-types.ts`)

The lifted vocabulary carries three conceptual groupings: the wire-type mirrors (~80 lines), the projection + thin helpers consumed by the moderator + participant (~180 lines), and the audience-specific flatten helpers + tuple shapes (~80 lines). Three options for module layout under `packages/shell/src/diagnostics/`:

- **(A — chosen)** Single `diagnostic-highlights.ts` carrying all three groupings, mirroring the participant's predecessor module shape (which already collapses the projection + thin helpers into one file). The audience-specific flatten helpers + tuple shapes append as a sibling block at the end of the same file, matching the audience's own predecessor module shape ([`apps/audience/src/graph/diagnosticHighlights.ts:249-331`](../../../apps/audience/src/graph/diagnosticHighlights.ts#L249) — same structure today).
- **(B)** Three-file split: `wire-types.ts` + `projection.ts` + `fire-tuples.ts`, each with its own barrel re-export. Cost: introduces an internal import graph between three module files (the projection file imports the wire types; the fire-tuples file imports the projection file's `affectedEntities`); each file's test suite has to set up the same payload fixtures; the consolidated coverage advantage (one suite, hand-build payloads once, exercise every helper against the same fixtures) fragments.
- **(C)** Two-file split: `diagnostic-highlights.ts` (wire types + projection + thin helpers) + `diagnostic-fire-tuples.ts` (audience-specific helpers + tuple shapes). Cost: a partial-fragmentation argument — has the same setup-duplication smell as option (B) without the conceptual clarity of full separation.

Chosen: (A). Mirrors both predecessor module shapes (the moderator + participant's full module is essentially this; the audience adds the fire-tuple block at the end of the same file). Consolidates the per-payload fixtures in a single test suite. The shell's convention so far has been "one module per conceptual vocabulary" (e.g. `axiom-marks/axiom-marks.ts` carries the four projection symbols + `AxiomMarkBadge.tsx` is a sibling component file — not because the projection is "different" from the badge but because the badge IS a React component with its own structural concerns; the projection helpers and the flatten helpers are both pure logic, so they belong in one file). A future split is cheap if the file grows past ~500 lines or if a new helper group requires it; today the lift consolidates ~340 lines into one home.

### §3 — Ship `createDefaultWsStoreInitializer()` alongside `createDefaultWsStore()`; per-app devtools wrappers stay per-app

The moderator + participant each currently call `create<WsState>()(withDevtools('<surface>/ws', (set) => ({...})))` — the local state-creator body knows about `withDevtools`. The audience calls `create<WsStoreState>()((set) => ({...}))` directly. Folding `activeDiagnostics` into the shell means the three local state-creator bodies collapse to "the shell's default state-creator, plus or minus devtools." Three options for how the shell exposes the factory:

- **(A — chosen)** The shell ships BOTH `createDefaultWsStore()` (returning a fresh wrapped store — the audience's path) AND `createDefaultWsStoreInitializer()` (returning the bare `StateCreator<BaseWsStoreState>` that callers wrap with middleware before passing to `create()` — the moderator + participant path). The moderator becomes `useWsStore = create<BaseWsStoreState>()(withDevtools('moderator/ws', createDefaultWsStoreInitializer()))`; the participant mirrors. The audience becomes `audienceWsStore = createDefaultWsStore()`.
- **(B)** The shell ships only `createDefaultWsStore()`; the moderator + participant reach into the shell for the *initializer* by calling `create<BaseWsStoreState>()(withDevtools('moderator/ws', (set) => createDefaultWsStore().getState() ... ))` or some other hack to recover the state-creator from the wrapped store. Cost: ugly, brittle, and the wrapped-vs-bare boundary is an internal shell concern that should not leak to callers.
- **(C)** The shell ships only the bare `createDefaultWsStoreInitializer()`; the audience wraps it with `create<BaseWsStoreState>()(createDefaultWsStoreInitializer())` inline. Cost: marginally more verbose for the audience (the one caller that doesn't need devtools), but symmetric with the other two. Defensible alternative — see "considered" below.
- **(D)** The shell ships a `withDevtools` middleware as part of the substrate; all three callers consume it from the shell. Cost: `withDevtools` is currently per-app because it gates on `import.meta.env.DEV` (a Vite compile-time constant unique to each app's build); moving it to the shell either (a) requires the shell to expose a `devtools(enabled: boolean)` factory the callers parameterize (an extra indirection) or (b) bakes a specific `import.meta.env.DEV` resolution into the shell that won't track each consumer's build correctly. The per-app shim is a small enough thing that leaving it in place is cleaner than centralizing it.

Chosen: (A). Both APIs ship — the audience gets a one-line constructor (`createDefaultWsStore()`); the moderator + participant get an initializer they can wrap with their own per-app devtools middleware. The two functions live in the same file, share their implementation (`createDefaultWsStore = () => create<BaseWsStoreState>()(createDefaultWsStoreInitializer())`), and the bare-vs-wrapped boundary is explicit in the API. Per-app `devtools.ts` wrappers stay per-app per option (D)'s rationale.

The asymmetry between the audience (one line) and moderator+participant (three lines) is intentional and matches the actual per-surface need: the audience does not surface devtools to its read-only broadcast viewers; the moderator + participant are interactive surfaces where the dev-build redux-devtools session is load-bearing. Forcing all three to the same shape would either inflate the audience's wsStore.ts unnecessarily or push devtools into the shell against (D)'s reasoning.

### §4 — Lift the WS-store `activeDiagnostics` slot canonically onto `BaseWsSessionState` (not as a parallel `RichWsSessionState` interface)

The predecessor `shell_substrate_extraction` Decision §"WsStore extraction shape" left the `activeDiagnostics` slot OUT of `BaseWsSessionState` and adopted path (b) — the moderator (and subsequently the participant + audience) extend `BaseWsSessionState` locally. Now that the three-caller convergence has fired, two options for how the slot becomes canonical:

- **(A — chosen)** Add `activeDiagnostics: ReadonlyMap<string, DiagnosticPayload>` directly to `BaseWsSessionState`. Every shell-default consumer of the store inherits the slot; the three per-app widenings collapse entirely. `lastDiagnostic` remains on the base as before (it predates this leaf and serves a slightly different purpose — "last envelope seen" — that one or two existing readers continue to consume).
- **(B)** Introduce a parallel `RichWsSessionState extends BaseWsSessionState` interface carrying `activeDiagnostics`, and have `createDefaultWsStore()` return a `WsStoreLike<RichWsSessionState>` while keeping `BaseWsSessionState` un-widened. Cost: this preserves "base = minimal contract; rich = projection-extended" as two-level type hierarchy but offers no actual flexibility — there is no caller today who wants the un-widened base, and any future caller who genuinely wanted a `lastDiagnostic`-only store could build their own state-creator without the `activeDiagnostics` slot directly; the two-level hierarchy in the shell would be a YAGNI abstraction. The `shell_substrate_extraction` predecessor's path (b) was explicitly conditioned on "we'll widen the base once the third caller materializes" — that condition has fired.

Chosen: (A). The `activeDiagnostics` slot has converged across all three callers' wsStores; the shape and dispatch rules are three-times-empirically-validated; the canonicalization is the predecessor's intended endgame. The widening is additive at the type level (no existing consumer is harmed — they all already widen this exact slot today) and consolidating to one interface reduces the cognitive surface for future shell consumers (one `BaseWsSessionState` to learn; no "but you might want `RichWsSessionState` instead" branching).

### §5 — Wire-type interfaces lift INTO the shell, NOT into `packages/shared-types`

The three predecessor copies each inline the per-kind wire-payload interfaces (`WireCycleDiagnostic`, `WireContradictionDiagnostic`, etc.) as a local mirror of the server's per-kind payload shapes. A reasonable alternative would be to promote the mirror into `packages/shared-types` so the server itself can export the per-kind shapes and every client (current + future) imports from the shared package, eliminating the mirror entirely.

Three options:

- **(A — chosen)** The wire-type interfaces lift into the shell (`packages/shell/src/diagnostics/diagnostic-highlights.ts`) as the consolidated client-side mirror. The server's [`apps/server/src/diagnostics/event-emission.ts`](../../../apps/server/src/diagnostics/event-emission.ts) + [`apps/server/src/diagnostics/coherency-hint-detection.ts`](../../../apps/server/src/diagnostics/coherency-hint-detection.ts) stay the authoritative source; the shell's mirror tracks them; the consolidated test suite is the regression pin for drift. Header docstring explicitly calls out the mirror-vs-server relationship.
- **(B)** Promote the per-kind interfaces into `packages/shared-types/src/ws-envelope.ts` (or a new sibling module under `packages/shared-types/src/diagnostics/`); the server's diagnostics modules import the canonical types from shared-types; every client imports from shared-types too; no client-side mirror anywhere. Cost: this is a strictly larger refactor (touches the server's diagnostic-emission code path; touches the wire envelope's `diagnostic: unknown` field — promoting to a typed discriminated union means the envelope shape narrows; the existing `unknown`-narrowing pattern at the per-app projection layer becomes redundant; the change has effects far beyond this leaf's scope). The promotion is its own architectural decision (deserves its own ADR; deserves a focused refinement) and conflating it with the shell-extraction lift would balloon the 1d effort estimate and entangle two independent concerns.
- **(C)** Leave the wire-type interfaces inline in `apps/server/**` AND in `packages/shell/src/diagnostics/` (i.e. accept that the server has the source, the shell has the mirror, and future shared-types promotion is a separate task). Same as option (A) essentially; the distinguishing element is whether the shell's docstring registers shared-types promotion as a future task.

Chosen: (A) with the explicit forward-reference in the header docstring. The shell's mirror today is a strict superset of what shared-types would carry (the shell's interfaces include the projection-side `DiagnosticHighlight` / `DiagnosticHighlightIndex` shapes that ARE client-side aggregation types, not wire types — those would never go to shared-types). Promoting just the wire-type subset to shared-types is a future cleanup that this leaf does NOT pre-register as a named-future-task because the value vs cost calculus warrants its own decision: the cross-workspace drift risk that the lift dissolves at the *client* layer (three callers → one shell mirror) is a much sharper risk than the residual server-vs-client mirror drift (one server source + one shell mirror), and the consolidated shell test suite is the regression pin for the server-vs-shell pair.

### §6 — Lift ALL participant-local thin helpers (`nodeHasDiagnostic`, `edgeHasDiagnostic`, `diagnosticSeverityFor`); none stay app-local

The participant's `diagnosticHighlights.ts` ships three thin helpers exclusive to it: `nodeHasDiagnostic(index, nodeId): boolean`, `edgeHasDiagnostic(index, edgeId): boolean`, and `diagnosticSeverityFor(index, target, id): DiagnosticHighlightSeverity | 'none'`. The participant's predecessor refinement Decision §8 documented that these were added "for future consumers" (`part_entity_detail_panel`, methodology-suggestions surfaces, axiom-mark-to-resolve-a-cycle gesture). The `shell_axiom_marks_extraction` precedent (Decision §1 there) kept the participant's `nodeHasAxiomMark` participant-local because it had a single boolean-collapse-shape call site neither cross-surface caller consumed.

Three options for the disposition of the three diagnostic thin helpers:

- **(A — chosen)** Lift all three to the shell alongside the rest of the vocabulary. They are pure logic (no React, no per-app coupling); they accept the shell-resident `DiagnosticHighlightIndex` (which lifts to the shell as part of `projectDiagnosticHighlights`'s output type); their footprint is small (each is a one-or-two-line wrapper around `index.nodes.has(id)` / `index.edges.has(id)` / a severity bucket lookup); and their semantic shape — presence/severity sentinels with the explicit `'none'` literal — is already aligned with future cross-surface consumers.
- **(B)** Keep them participant-local; the participant's `diagnosticHighlights.ts` collapses to a re-export shim around the lifted helpers + the three local thin helpers stay defined (the `shell_axiom_marks_extraction` Decision §5 pattern). Cost: the participant's `diagnosticHighlights.ts` survives as a shim only so it can carry three trivial wrappers; future moderator + audience consumers of the same shape would either re-implement the trivial wrappers (drift risk for `'none'`-vs-`undefined` divergence) or have to import from the participant workspace (architecturally awkward — the same problem `extract_facet_pill` solved by lifting the workspace edge).
- **(C)** Inline the three helpers at their call sites; delete them entirely. Cost: the predecessor refinement's Decision §8 explicitly anticipated future consumers; deleting the helpers contradicts that intent and would force every future consumer to either re-derive the trivial wrappers (drift risk per option B) or live with the awkward `index.nodes.has(id)` / `index.edges.has(id)` pattern at every call site.

Chosen: (A). The helpers are tiny, cross-surface-useful, no per-app coupling. Mirrors the `shell_axiom_marks_extraction` precedent's INVERSE: there, the helper was kept participant-local because it had a boolean-collapse shape neither other surface consumed; here, the helpers are general-purpose presence/severity sentinels every cross-surface consumer would want. The `aud_diagnostic_fire_animation` predecessor's overlay (and any future per-entity diagnostic panel on the audience) is a natural future consumer; the moderator's [`DiagnosticSuggestionsPanel.tsx`](../../../apps/moderator/src/layout/DiagnosticSuggestionsPanel.tsx) already reads severity off `DiagnosticPayload` directly but could plausibly be refactored to consume `diagnosticSeverityFor` for index-rooted lookups. No participant-local survivor needed.

The participant's `diagnosticHighlights.ts` deletes cleanly (no shim) because no helper stays behind. Consistent with the `extract_facet_pill` precedent's full-deletion path (no shim) for files where every symbol lifts.

### §7 — Root re-export from `@a-conversa/shell`, not a subpath export

Every prior shell extraction (`shell_substrate_extraction`, `extract_facet_pill`, `shell_axiom_marks_extraction`, `extract_pending_axiom_mark_projector`, `extract_cytoscape_projectors`, `extract_votes_by_facet_projector`, `shared_shell_extract_merge_slots_and_derive_slot_occupants`) re-exports from the root and consumers import from `@a-conversa/shell` directly. Two options:

- **(A — chosen)** Re-export every lifted symbol from `packages/shell/src/index.ts`. Consumers import via `import { projectDiagnosticHighlights, diagnosticIdentityKey, ... } from '@a-conversa/shell'`.
- **(B)** Add a subpath export entry `"./diagnostics"` to `packages/shell/package.json`'s `exports` map; consumers import via `from '@a-conversa/shell/diagnostics'`. Cost: introduces a manifest-maintenance edge that no prior shell extraction needed; the consumer's import path becomes verbose for no offsetting benefit.

Chosen: (A). Honors the convention every prior shell extraction established; keeps the consumer import path uniform across all shell-provided symbols.

### §8 — No new Playwright / Cucumber coverage (Vitest + existing integration tests are sufficient)

Three observations frame this:

1. This task is a pure file-location refactor + WS-store-slot consolidation. No user-visible behavior changes; no protocol seam crossed; no projector output shifts (the audience's overlay, the participant's graph view, the moderator's canvas / suggestions panel all consume the same symbols with the same semantics post-lift). The five existing per-surface test suites that exercise the helpers + the `applyDiagnostic` reducer (moderator `DiagnosticSuggestionsPanel.test.tsx` + moderator `selectors.test.ts` + participant `GraphView`-rendering tests + audience `DiagnosticFireOverlay.test.tsx` + audience `DiagnosticEdgeFireOverlay.test.tsx`) continue passing against the lifted symbols — that is the structural regression pin for the import-path rewire.
2. The task lives under `shell_package.*`, not under any of the UI-stream groups (`moderator_ui.*`, `participant_ui.*`, `audience.*`, `replay_test.*`) that the orchestrator brief's UI-stream e2e policy applies to. The shell-package extraction does not change reachability for any surface; no new route lights up, no new event surface fires.
3. The cross-surface Cucumber rule applies to wire / broadcast / projector changes observable at the system seam. None apply here — the server's `apps/server/**` is untouched, the wire envelope is untouched, the broadcast shape is untouched.

Three options:

- **(A — chosen)** Vitest at the canonical home (`packages/shell/src/diagnostics/diagnostic-highlights.test.ts` + the `applyDiagnostic` cases added to `packages/shell/src/ws/ws-client.test.ts`) plus the five existing per-surface tests continuing to pass. No new Playwright. No new Cucumber.
- **(B)** Scope a Playwright spec that drives a moderator session through a fired-cycle scenario and confirms the suggestions panel + canvas highlight post-lift. Cost: redundant with the moderator's existing `DiagnosticSuggestionsPanel.test.tsx` Vitest coverage at the component layer + the existing moderator/participant e2e specs that exercise the surrounding feature; pure refactor adds no new e2e debt.
- **(C)** Scope a Cucumber scenario for the `applyDiagnostic` active-set behaviour at the wire boundary. Cost: Cucumber is the wire / broadcast / projector seam pin per ADR 0021; the `activeDiagnostics` slot is a client-side projection of wire envelopes, not a wire / broadcast surface itself. The wire side (server → client envelope) is already pinned at the server layer; the client side (envelope → projected state) is exactly what `packages/shell/src/ws/ws-client.test.ts` tests at the unit layer. Vitest is the architecturally-correct pin.

Chosen: (A). The Vitest layer is already at full coverage from the three predecessor suites' union; consolidating it at the shell home preserves coverage and centralizes future-maintenance work. The existing per-surface tests continuing to pass post-rewire is the structural regression pin; no new e2e scope is owed.
