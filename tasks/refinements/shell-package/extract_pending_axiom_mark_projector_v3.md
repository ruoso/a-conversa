## Why it needs to be done

The v2 audit closed with the explicit pre-registration of THIS `_v3` task at unchanged trigger semantics (both UI leaves `complete 100`). The orchestrator's pick-task pass surfaced this task on cadence. The pass cannot verify whether the participant + audience leaves have materialized between v2's commit (2026-05-28) and this `_v3`'s commit (2026-05-29) ŌĆö that is the refinement-stage activity this leaf delivers.

Four observations make the audit valuable (rather than a no-op formality at one further iteration's distance from the originating v1):

1. **The v2 audit identified the same revision path the v1 audit named (the participant and audience refinements would have to explicitly revise their principled-absence decisions), and one further audit-iteration is the right place to re-check whether that path has been walked.** A search of the two work-streams' refinements + their `.tji` task blocks at v3 commit time shows zero revisions: [`part_axiom_mark_decoration.md`](../participant-ui/part_axiom_mark_decoration.md) Status line says "Done ŌĆö 2026-05-17" with no amendments; [`aud_axiom_mark_decoration.md`](../audience/aud_axiom_mark_decoration.md) Status line says "Done ŌĆö 2026-05-28" with no amendments. No new participant leaf scoping graph-canvas pending-axiom-mark rendering; no new audience leaf scoping `aud_axiom_mark_pending_render` or any analog; no commits touching either surface's stance on pending-axiom-marks. The principled-absence is durable across two audit iterations, not transient.
2. **The sibling `extract_votes_by_facet_projector_v2` lift remains the load-bearing asymmetry citation.** The v2 audit's Decision ┬¦1 recorded in full why that `_v2` lifted (in-flight methodology task closed the divergence half; two byte-identical projector bodies across two existing callers gave evidence weight to relaxing the third-workspace half) and why this audit chain's pending-axiom-mark `_v2` did NOT (the methodology is settled in the principled-absence direction; the trigger is genuinely binary on UI work that is not scheduled). The asymmetry is unchanged at `_v3` audit time and continues to be the durable reason this chain's `_vN` continues to defer while the votes-by-facet `_v2` terminated by lifting. The audit records this so a future reader sees at one glance why the two chains diverged at this disposition step without having to re-derive the methodology context from the source refinements.
3. **Closing this `_v3` leaf cleanly prevents pick-task queue churn at the audit cadence.** Leaving the leaf open on the orchestrator's queue without a fresh audit means every pick-task pass either re-refines it (refinement-document churn for no new content) or skips it ambiguously. The closer marks the leaf `complete 100`; the future-trigger work is held by a freshly-named `_v4` follow-up (Decision ┬¦4) whose trigger semantics are identical to `_v3`'s. If the trigger fires before the next audit cadence, the implementer reads `_v4` directly; if not, the next audit refines `_v4` the same way this audit refines `_v3`.
4. **The audit chain extending past two iterations is itself a signal.** The v1 audit (2026-05-28) and v2 audit (2026-05-28) closed on the same day under the original-WBS-cadence-burst; v3 (2026-05-29) is the first iteration that confirms the deferral holds across **calendar-time** as well as audit-iteration-count. The chain is now long enough to start asking *meta-level* questions ŌĆö has the orchestrator's pick-task pass surfaced this leaf because of a genuine new datum, or because the queue is otherwise quiescent? ŌĆö without yet being long enough to warrant changing the cadence (Decision ┬¦3 still chooses close-and-defer over rename / drop). If `_v4` and `_v5` close with identical findings, the next audit may revisit the cadence-rate itself; today's audit holds the cadence at one-per-orchestrator-pick-pass.
