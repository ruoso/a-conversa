## Why it needs to be done

The v1 audit closed with the explicit pre-registration of THIS `_v2` task at sharpened trigger semantics (both UI leaves `complete 100`, rather than the looser "second + third caller materialize" framing in `extract_cytoscape_projectors` Decision §3). The orchestrator's pick-task pass surfaced this task on cadence. The pass cannot verify whether the participant + audience leaves have materialized between v1's commit (2026-05-28) and this `_v2`'s commit (also 2026-05-28 in the current cadence, but the audit-iteration distinguishes them) — that is the refinement-stage activity this leaf delivers.

Three observations make the audit valuable (rather than a no-op formality, even at one iteration's distance from v1):

1. **The v1 audit identified a specific future revision path (the participant and audience refinements would have to explicitly revise their principled-absence decisions), and the audit window is the right place to check whether that path has been walked.** A search of the two work-streams' refinements + their `.tji` task blocks at v2 commit time shows zero revisions: no new participant leaf scoping graph-canvas pending-axiom-mark rendering; no new audience leaf scoping `aud_axiom_mark_pending_render` or any analog; no commits touching either surface's stance on pending-axiom-marks. The principled-absence is durable, not transient.
2. **The sibling `extract_votes_by_facet_projector_v2` lift (settled 2026-05-28 commit 8c7087b) shows what a `_v2` task does when its preconditions DO converge — and explicitly highlights the asymmetry with this case.** That lift fired because [`align_vote_facet_target_vocabulary`](../data-and-methodology/align_vote_facet_target_vocabulary.md) closed the methodology-shaped half of its preconditions and its Decision §1 relaxed the "third workspace" half on the strength of two byte-identical post-alignment projector bodies. For pending-axiom-marks there is no analogous methodology task in-flight (the methodology is settled in the principled-absence direction at the moderator level); the trigger remains genuinely binary on UI work that is not scheduled. The audit records this asymmetry so a future reader can see at a glance why one `_v2` lifted and the other did not — without having to re-derive the methodology context.
3. **Closing this `_v2` leaf cleanly prevents pick-task queue churn at the audit cadence.** Leaving the leaf open on the orchestrator's queue without a fresh audit means every pick-task pass either re-refines it (refinement-document churn for no new content) or skips it ambiguously. The closer marks the leaf `complete 100`; the future-trigger work is held by a freshly-named `_v3` follow-up (Decision §4) whose trigger semantics are identical to `_v2`'s. If the trigger fires before the next audit cadence, the implementer reads `_v3` directly; if not, the next audit refines `_v3` the same way this audit refines `_v2`.

The follow-on benefits of the audit:

- **Prevents premature ossification.** The three-caller policy's whole point is to wait for empirical convergence rather than to design-shape-and-hope. Lifting `projectPendingAxiomMarks` into the shell today as a single-caller move "in case" the second and third callers eventually materialize would create a shell-side projector whose only consumer is the moderator's `GraphCanvasPane` — exactly the anti-pattern `extract_cytoscape_projectors` Decision §1 rejected for the participant boolean+count helpers and that `extract_facet_pill` Decision §2 set the three-caller policy to prevent. The v1 audit rejected this for the same reasons; the `_v2` re-audit confirms the reasoning still holds.
- **Records the named-future-task path crisply at the audit-iteration cadence.** The orchestrator brief's tech-debt registration policy asks every deferred task to be named with effort + description; Decision §4 supplies both for the `_v3` follow-up, freshly stating the trigger semantics one audit-iteration further out.
- **Pins the recipe in advance.** When the trigger does fire (likely 2-4 quarters out, after the participant's pending-proposals pane expands or the audience's broadcast-feedback iteration surfaces a need), the implementer of `_v3` reads this audit and the v1 audit and follows the `shell_axiom_marks_extraction` + `extract_cytoscape_projectors` + `extract_votes_by_facet_projector_v2` recipe verbatim — no design re-derivation needed across the audit chain.
- **Closes the audit-iteration chain cleanly at one increment per orchestrator-pick cadence.** Each audit-iteration leaf (`_v1`, `_v2`, `_v3`, …) corresponds to one orchestrator-pick pass; the audit is the per-iteration deliverable; the lift is the terminating non-audit deliverable when the trigger finally fires. The cadence is the same one [`extract_votes_by_facet_projector_v2`](extract_votes_by_facet_projector_v2.md) followed (its audit-iteration terminated by lifting; this one continues by re-deferring).

The salient new datum at `_v2` audit time is the sibling [`extract_votes_by_facet_projector_v2.md`](extract_votes_by_facet_projector_v2.md) lift (settled 2026-05-28 at commit 8c7087b): that `_v2` task RELAXED its third-workspace precondition on the strength of an in-flight methodology task ([`align_vote_facet_target_vocabulary`](../data-and-methodology/align_vote_facet_target_vocabulary.md)) that closed the methodology-divergence half AND produced two byte-identical projector bodies across two existing callers (moderator + participant). The asymmetry with pending-axiom-marks is structural and must be made explicit, because a future implementer reading this refinement deserves to see why the two `_v2` tasks diverged at exactly this disposition step:

- The votes-by-facet projector had two callers with a methodology-shaped divergence. Resolving the methodology produced two existing callers in lockstep. The "third workspace" criterion was the weak link; relaxing it cost nothing because the strength-of-evidence was already at two byte-identical callers.
- The pending-axiom-marks projector has ONE caller — by design. The participant + audience surfaces have explicitly decided not to render pending-axiom-marks. There is no in-flight methodology task; the methodology has already concluded that pending-axiom-marks belong at the proposal-author surface only. Relaxing the trigger here means lifting a one-caller projector into shell on the strength of a methodology decision that says no second caller exists. That is the opposite of the votes-by-facet relaxation rationale — there the methodology resolved a divergence and produced two converged callers; here the methodology has produced one principled-absence and one principled-absence on either side of the moderator.

Three options for what to do this turn:

- **(A — chosen)** Defer again. Mark this `_v2` leaf `complete 100`; register `_v3` with identical trigger semantics (both UI leaves `complete 100`). The lift continues to wait for both UI surfaces to revise their principled-absence decisions and ship concrete rendering. Reasoning: the trigger is binary on UI work that is not scheduled and is not in-flight; deferring against an unscheduled trigger is the right call exactly because the alternative (lifting on the strength of a methodology decision that excludes the additional callers) is worst-case YAGNI inversion. The audit is the deliverable for this iteration; the lift is the deliverable when (and if) the principled-absence reverses.
- **(B)** Relax the trigger on the votes-by-facet precedent — lift now, with one workspace caller (moderator), under the rationale that "the projector's coupling to ADR 0030 §9 makes it shell-shaped" or "the recipe is mechanically trivial." Cost: introduces a single-caller shell entry with one workspace user — the anti-pattern the v1 audit Decision §1 alternative B already rejected on the same reasoning; the sibling `_v2`'s relaxation is not a precedent for THIS lift because the structural conditions are inverted (there: methodology-divergence resolution producing two convergent callers; here: methodology-explicit principled-absence preventing additional callers). The strength of the votes-by-facet evidence was two byte-identical post-alignment bodies; here there is one body. Rejected.
- **(C)** Defer indefinitely AND drop the `_v3` registration — let this leaf close with no follow-up, and let any future re-opening of the participant or audience stance trigger a fresh `tasks/27-shell-package.tji` task from scratch. Cost: loses the citable audit chain that links every iteration's findings; the orchestrator's tech-debt registration policy asks for a crisp future-task; dropping the registration violates that policy. Future implementers re-discovering the deferral from cold would have to re-derive the recipe + the rejection of relaxation alternatives without the audit chain to reference. Rejected.

Chosen: (A). The three-caller policy floor is three; the current caller count is one; the methodology's authority for additional callers is exclusionary, not enabling.
