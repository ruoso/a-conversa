## Why it needs to be done

The orchestrator pick-task pass surfaced this leaf because its predecessor edges (`part_entity_detail_panel_chromatic_axiom_mark_badge`, `audience.aud_graph_rendering`) both settled. The pick-task pass cannot itself verify whether the named lift has already been performed under a different task name — that is a refinement-stage activity. This audit is that activity.

Three observations make the audit valuable (rather than a no-op formality):

1. **The lift this task names was deliberately bundled into a sibling task at the predecessor's close.** [`aud_axiom_mark_decoration.md` Decision §3](../audience/aud_axiom_mark_decoration.md) — the predecessor that materialized the third caller — explicitly chose to **register the consolidation as `shell_axiom_marks_extraction` rather than `extract_axiom_mark_badge`** because the badge is structurally inseparable from the `axiomMarks.ts` projection vocabulary it consumes (`AxiomMark` is the badge's prop type; `projectAxiomMarks` is what produces the values the badge renders). Lifting badge and projection in one atomic commit was the cleaner architectural seam; splitting them across two WBS leaves would have either (a) created an awkward ordering dependency (badge depends on projection being in shell; projection depends on convergence) or (b) duplicated the per-locale Vitest matrix unification work. The sibling task name was the right choice; this leaf's name is the *registered placeholder* the orchestrator surfaced when the trigger fired. Closing this leaf cleanly records that the placeholder's work shipped under the sibling name.

2. **The participant detail-panel badge's prop-shape divergence is methodology-shaped, not cosmetic.** The participant's panel badge takes `{ participantId, screenName }` because the participant has the roster locally; the moderator's canvas badge took `{ mark: AxiomMark }` because the moderator has the projection but not (today) a participants projection. The shell's consolidated badge accepts an **optional `screenName: string`** so both call sites can be expressed through one component, with conditional i18n that disambiguates "show UUID" (moderator) from "show screen name" (participant panel). This is a behaviour-changing decision — not the kind of decision a shell-package mechanical-refactor audit can make. The right home for it is a dedicated refinement (`shell_axiom_mark_panel_badge_consolidation`) that owns the prop-shape, i18n, and testid migrations as a coherent whole. The present audit confirms that home exists.

3. **Closing this leaf cleanly prevents pick-task queue churn.** Leaving `extract_axiom_mark_badge` open on the orchestrator's pick-task queue without an audit means every orchestrator pass picks it up and re-refines it; refinement-document churn produces nothing new. Worse, a future implementer might attempt the lift, discover the moderator + audience lift has already shipped, and then face an ambiguous question ("does this leaf still own the participant panel consolidation, or is that `shell_axiom_mark_panel_badge_consolidation`'s job?"). The audit settles the ambiguity in advance and closes the leaf.

The follow-on benefits of the audit:

- **Prevents accidental re-extraction.** A future contributor reading the `.tji` task note ("Lift the moderator's `apps/moderator/src/graph/AxiomMarkBadge.tsx` and the participant's `apps/participant/src/detail/AxiomMarkBadge.tsx` into `packages/shell/src/facet-pill/` (or a sibling directory)") and not consulting refinement state could begin re-implementing the lift before realizing both source files are already (a) deleted from the moderator, (b) still present at the participant but tracked by a separate consolidation leaf. The Status block here captures the disposition citably.
- **Records cross-task accounting cleanly.** The audit's `Status` block names the sibling task that actually shipped the lift and the named-future-task that holds the residual. Future refinements (or orchestrator passes) citing this leaf get the right pointers in one read.
- **Confirms the three-caller-policy precedent applied correctly.** The audit traces the trigger from `extract_facet_pill` (the policy's source) through `part_axiom_mark_decoration` (the second caller) through `aud_axiom_mark_decoration` (the third caller's materialization + the deferred-consolidation registration) to `shell_axiom_marks_extraction` (the actual lift). Recording that chain makes the precedent reusable for future shell-package extractions whose triggers fire in a similar bundled-with-sibling shape.

Cost: duplicates the scope of `shell_axiom_mark_panel_badge_consolidation`, whose refinement holds the load-bearing Decisions on the prop-shape + i18n + testid migrations. Pre-empting those Decisions from a shell-package audit refinement inverts the normal task-shape direction (a consolidation refinement decides its behaviour change; an audit refinement decides what work belongs where). If the consolidation refinement's Decisions are wrong, the right fix is amending them — not absorbing them into a sibling audit. Rejected.

Chosen: (A). Honors the orchestrator's tech-debt registration policy (the shipped-lift is cited; the residual's home is cited); closes this leaf cleanly; keeps the audit findings in a single, citable place.
