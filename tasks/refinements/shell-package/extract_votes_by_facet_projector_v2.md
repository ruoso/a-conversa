## Why it needs to be done

After the alignment task ([`align_vote_facet_target_vocabulary.md`](../data-and-methodology/align_vote_facet_target_vocabulary.md), settled 2026-05-28), both client projectors are byte-identical modulo the participant's self-filter and the local `FacetTarget` type alias. The cost of leaving the two-copy duplication in place is the standard cross-surface drift risk this shell-package work-stream exists to eliminate:

- A future change to the per-`(entity, facet)` vote-bucketing semantics (a new facet kind per a future ADR widening of `FacetName`; a refinement to the at-insertion overwrite-in-place idiom; a new `target`-discriminated arm in the vote payload; a clarification to the unknown-proposal silent-drop posture) has to be applied in two files in two different apps with two different test suites. Any update that lands in one but not the other silently desynchronizes the moderator's and participant's vote displays — exactly the bug class the alignment task was scoped to prevent at the methodology layer, applied recursively at the projector-body layer.
- The participant's `projectOtherVotesByFacet` is structurally an artifact of historical port-and-divergence: the file was created by `part_other_vote_indicators` as a "port + filter self" of the moderator's `projectVotesByFacet`; its body diverged in proposal-kind vocabulary (closed by the alignment task) and in the unimportant detail of the `FacetTarget` type alias (closed by this lift). Post-lift the participant module is no longer load-bearing — its only purpose was to host a self-filtered variant, which the shell now hosts canonically.
- The duplication adds ~140 lines of client-bundle weight per surface (the projector body + dispatcher + types). Both surfaces already ship `@a-conversa/shell` as a runtime dependency (ADR 0026 / `shell_substrate_extraction`); consolidation moves the code into the shared chunk without adding a new dependency edge.

The three-caller-or-defer policy applied by `extract_facet_pill.md` Decision §2 and the predecessor audit's Decision §1 was the right call **for the methodology-divergence case**: at two callers with divergent vocabularies the API shape would have ossified around the divergence, either via a callback-shaped dispatcher (rejected as alternative C in the audit) or via a union-vocabulary projector that silently expanded vote-counts (rejected as alternative B). Both rejection paths assumed the methodology question would remain unresolved. The alignment task resolved it. The policy's "wait for empirical convergence" pretext is satisfied: convergence is empirical at the level of both projector BODIES (they are byte-identical after the alignment), even though only two workspaces consume the post-alignment shape today.

Holding the lift back for a third *workspace* would mean deferring a mechanically trivial refactor against a future audience consumer that may never materialize (the audience surface today renders rolled-up status pills via `PerFacetPillOverlay` — the design may legitimately stay at the status-pill level and never grow per-voter dot rows). That's the YAGNI inversion: the lift now is cheap and removes a real drift surface; the lift-when-a-third-workspace-shows-up policy hedges against a speculative consumer at the cost of permanent duplication. Decision §1 chooses the lift; alternatives (defer-again, lift-with-callback) are revisited and rejected.

The follow-on benefits:

- **One source of truth for the client-side per-(entity, facet) vote projector.** A future methodology change edits one `packages/shell/src/votes-by-facet/` block instead of two workspaces. The next surface to land per-facet vote rendering (audience, replay-test, root-app composite) imports from `@a-conversa/shell` directly — no cross-workspace port, no fourth-caller registration.
- **Test consolidation.** Two Vitest suites covering the same accumulator-loop semantics (twenty-three cases after the alignment task's additions) collapse into one. The self-filter variant's three new alignment-era cases (j / k / l in `otherVotesByFacet.test.ts`) compose into the same describe-block hierarchy as the moderator's analogous cases without duplicating the seed-log scaffolding.
- **Removes the participant's load-bearing port-comment debt.** Today the participant's [`otherVotesByFacet.ts:69`](../../../apps/participant/src/proposals/otherVotesByFacet.ts#L69) carries the "Mirrors the moderator's `projectVotesByFacet` verbatim except for the self-filter" claim — accurate post-alignment, but only enforced by reviewer attention. After the lift the invariant is enforced by the type system: one shared private loop, two thin public entry points (one with the filter, one without).
- **Pattern continuity.** This is the fourth shell-package projector / rule-set consolidation in 2026-05-28's cadence (`shell_axiom_marks_extraction`, `extract_cytoscape_projectors`, `extract_facet_status_rules` shipped 2026-05-28; this leaf is the fourth). Same recipe; consistency reduces review friction; future readers of the votes-by-facet module find the same sibling-directory + barrel + root-export shape as the other ten substrate subsystems.
- **Closes the predecessor audit's deferred-debt chain cleanly.** The audit (settled 2026-05-28) closed with explicit registration of this task. The alignment task closed precondition (1). The lift closes the chain.

## Decisions

### §1 — Lift now; the three-caller-or-defer policy's preconditions are met in spirit even though only two workspaces consume the post-alignment shape

The predecessor audit ([`extract_votes_by_facet_projector.md`](extract_votes_by_facet_projector.md) Decision §1) deferred the lift because the participant/moderator divergence was methodology-shaped (the proposal-kind vocabularies disagreed). Decision §4 there pre-registered THIS task with the trigger "fires when BOTH (a) `align_vote_facet_target_vocabulary` is `complete 100` AND (b) a third caller (audience surface, or replay-test, or root-app composite) lands per-facet-vote rendering against the now-aligned projector." Precondition (a) is closed (commit c79d369, 2026-05-28); precondition (b) is structurally NOT met (no audience / replay-test / root-app caller renders per-facet votes today; the audience surface paints rolled-up facet-status pills via [`PerFacetPillOverlay`](../../../apps/audience/src/graph/PerFacetPillOverlay.tsx), not per-voter dots; the design may legitimately stay at the status-pill level indefinitely).

The orchestrator + the alignment refinement Decision §6 settled that the third-caller-policy's *purpose* is met. The policy exists to defend against ossifying an API shape that turns out to be wrong; the audit's Decision §1 rejected callback-shaped and union-vocabulary alternatives precisely because the underlying methodology question was unresolved. The alignment task resolved it. The projector bodies are now byte-identical between the moderator and the participant (modulo the participant's at-insertion self-filter and the local type alias for `FacetTarget`). Lifting them now ossifies a *known-correct* API shape, not a speculative one.

Three options for what to do this turn:

- **(A — chosen)** Lift now. Both projectors land in shell as `projectVotesByFacet(events)` and `projectOtherVotesByFacet(events, currentParticipantId)`. The audit's deferred-debt chain closes. The lift is mechanically trivial (verbatim port + ~14 import rewires); the shape is empirically validated by both client surfaces consuming a byte-identical body. Reasoning: the three-caller policy's underlying concern (ossifying the wrong shape) was the methodology divergence the alignment task resolved; with that resolved, the policy's strict third-WORKSPACE requirement reduces to "wait for a speculative consumer that may never appear" — which is YAGNI inverted (the cost of waiting is permanent duplication; the cost of lifting is the diff in this task, ~14 file touches). The alignment task's own Decision §6 anticipates this leaf with the verbatim port language; honoring that frames this task as the natural continuation rather than a precondition-skip.
- **(B)** Defer again until a real third-WORKSPACE caller appears. Cost: indefinite hold on a mechanically trivial refactor; permanent two-copy duplication of a now-byte-identical projector body; future methodology / per-facet vote rendering refinements pay the drift-risk tax with no offsetting benefit. The audit's reason for deferral (methodology divergence) is gone; the remaining reason (audience-caller as evidence of shape stability) is hypothetical and weak — the participant's verbatim port post-alignment is already strong evidence (two of two callers agree byte-for-byte). Rejected; defers indefinitely for marginal additional evidence at high cost.
- **(C)** Lift the canonical projector only (`projectVotesByFacet`) and leave the participant's `projectOtherVotesByFacet` as a workspace-local wrapper that calls into shell + applies a post-filter. Cost: defeats the lift's purpose (the duplication is the projector body, not the public-API surface); requires the participant to either re-implement the loop (returning duplication) or take a less-efficient post-filter pass over the moderator's full output. Rejected as the worst of both worlds — partial deduplication with worst-case runtime characteristics.

Chosen: (A). The lift is the right call because the methodology question is settled, the projector bodies are byte-identical, and the cost of indefinite deferral exceeds any incremental confidence a third-WORKSPACE caller might provide.

### §5 — Two public entry points (`projectVotesByFacet` + `projectOtherVotesByFacet`); shared private loop; no options-bag merge

The shell's votes-by-facet module exposes two entry points with distinct signatures. Internally they share a private `walkVotes(events, currentParticipantId: string | null)` helper that runs the accumulator loop once; the no-filter entry point passes `null`, the self-filter variant passes the participant id. Three options for the public-API shape:

- **(A — chosen)** Two entry points (`projectVotesByFacet(events)` + `projectOtherVotesByFacet(events, currentParticipantId)`), shared private loop. Reasoning: matches both predecessor signatures verbatim — no consumer rewires its call site, only its import; the at-insertion self-filter is preserved (drop votes by `currentParticipantId` before any allocation, not after the fact); the two entry points name two semantically distinct projections (the no-filter form is "all per-facet votes"; the self-filter form is "all per-facet votes by OTHER participants — the canonical participant-side view"). Clear at the call site; minimum-scope at the implementation.
- **(B)** One entry point with an options bag: `projectVotesByFacet(events, options?: { exclude?: string })`. The participant calls with `{ exclude: currentParticipantId }`.
