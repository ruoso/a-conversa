## What this task is

Audit `contradiction_detection` against the question *"should this diagnostic surface findings on annotation-endpoint contradicts edges?"* and pin the answer with explicit methodology grounding.

The parent audit `diagnostics_annotation_endpoint_semantics_audit` ran the same audit against the other five structural diagnostics (cycle, dangling-claim, coherency-hint, multi-warrant, pending-consequences) and pinned a per-diagnostic keep-skip default. It carved `contradiction_detection` out of scope per its D8 because the methodology doc didn't yet enumerate the contradicts-vs-annotation semantics. The sibling `coherency_self_referential_annotation_contradicts_rule` task then landed the missing methodology paragraph at [`docs/methodology.md` L236](../../../docs/methodology.md). This audit closes the parent D8 with the methodology citation now in place.

Concretely the deliverable is:

1. **The audit decision** (D1 below): `contradiction_detection` keeps its skip on annotation-endpoint edges across all three shapes (node-source / annotation-target, annotation-source / node-target, annotation-source / annotation-target) and both anchor configurations (self-referential, cross-anchor). The methodology citation at L236 is the authoritative grounding: contradicts is a substance-layer relation between peer entities, not between an entity and its commentary. The detector is classified as **blocking** per `blocking_vs_advisory_classification`; surfacing a blocking finding on a configuration the methodology positions as a category error would block forward progress on the wrong layer.

2. **Source-comment update** at [`contradiction-detection.ts:108-112`](../../../apps/server/src/diagnostics/contradiction-detection.ts), replacing the predecessor's `D4` back-link with one that cites this refinement's D1 plus the methodology line range. No control-flow change.

The audit adds a new section pinning `detectContradictions(projection).length === 0` across all three endpoint shapes × both anchor configurations, in both edge directions where applicable. Approximately six new cases (see Acceptance).

4. **WBS follow-up registration** (D3): one named candidate-future coherency-hint rule for the **non-self-referential** annotation-endpoint contradicts subset that the existing `self-referential-annotation-contradicts` rule does not reach. The cross-anchor subset (the walkthrough's E15 case, `N19 contradicts A2` where `A2` annotates `N6`, not `N19`) is methodologically the same category error as the self-referential case per L236 but is not surfaced by any rule today.

## Decisions

- **D1 — Keep skip. Methodology positions `contradicts` as a peer-entity relation; `contradiction_detection` is blocking; the rule-4 target-substance check structurally cannot fire on an annotation-target.** Rationale:
  - **Direct methodology citation.** [`docs/methodology.md` L236](../../../docs/methodology.md): *"the methodology positions `contradicts` as a substance-layer relation between peer entities, not between an entity and its commentary."* This sentence resolves the gap the parent audit D8 flagged. It speaks to the *general* principle (peer-entity), broader than the self-referential anchor configuration the surrounding rule's shape covers — so it grounds the keep-skip across **both** self-referential and cross-anchor annotation-endpoint subsets.
  - **Classification implication.** Per [`blocking_vs_advisory_classification.md`](./blocking_vs_advisory_classification.md) and [`docs/methodology.md` L219](../../../docs/methodology.md), `contradiction_detection` is **blocking**. A blocking finding requires the moderator to acknowledge (resolve, accept-as-bedrock, or otherwise engage with) the contradiction before forward progress. Surfacing a blocking finding on a configuration the methodology treats as a category error would force the moderator through resolution machinery that doesn't apply (the L226 resolution paths — decompose, amend-node, axiom-mark — all operate on nodes; an annotation-endpoint contradicts edge has no node-pair to drive them).
  - **Structural redundancy confirms the skip.** The detector's rule-4 target-substance check at [`contradiction-detection.ts:129-132`](../../../apps/server/src/diagnostics/contradiction-detection.ts) calls `targetSubstanceFires(projection, edge.targetNodeId)`. With `targetNodeId === null` (the annotation-target case), the call is malformed by construction — annotations have no substance facet (`ProjectedAnnotation` lacks a `substanceFacet` field per [`projection/types.ts:240-260`](../../../apps/server/src/projection/types.ts)). The early skip at L108–112 is defence-in-depth; even if removed, rule 4 would still gate the fire on `null` substance state. The redundancy is honest — both gates pin the same structural truth from different angles.
  - **Test pin.** Six broadened Vitest cases (see Acceptance) cover all three endpoint shapes × both anchor configurations × both edge directions where applicable. Each asserts zero findings.
  - **Alternative considered: lift the skip and let rule 4 do the gating alone.** Rejected — defence-in-depth is the predecessor's established pattern across all six detectors; removing the early skip in just this one would be a stylistic inconsistency for no behavioural gain.
  - **Alternative considered: surface annotation-endpoint contradicts as a *new* `contradiction_detection` finding-kind variant (e.g., `AnnotationEndpointContradiction`) classified as advisory rather than blocking.** Rejected — would split the detector's output along a methodology-shape axis that no consumer downstream is asking for; the advisory case is the exact responsibility of `coherency_hint_detection`, and the parent audit's D3 precedent puts annotation-endpoint advisory smells in that detector. Adding a second advisory channel duplicates the seam.
  - **Alternative considered: keep skip but split the audit conclusion by anchor configuration (self-referential vs cross-anchor) into separate decisions.** Rejected at this layer — the methodology citation at L236 grounds both subsets identically; splitting the decision would imply they have separate rationales when they don't. Anchor-configuration distinction matters for the *advisory* follow-up (per D3 — only the cross-anchor subset needs a new rule because the self-referential subset is already covered), not for this audit's keep-skip conclusion.

- **D2 — Synthetic minimal fixtures; no walkthrough E15 dependency.** Rationale:
  - **The parent audit's precedent.** Each of the parent audit's broadened Vitest cases used a minimal projection (one or two nodes + the edges-of-interest), independent of the walkthrough fixture.
