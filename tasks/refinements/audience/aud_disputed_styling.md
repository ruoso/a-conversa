## What this task is

The third per-state leaf on the audience's Cytoscape canvas — nodes and edges whose card-level rollup is `'disputed'` (at least one current participant has voted `dispute` on a facet, blocking commit) get a red border / line treatment that reads as "this needs resolution" on the broadcast surface. This completes the three baseline agreement-layer states (proposed / agreed / disputed) viewers need to read on the broadcast canvas, with the fourth agreement-layer state (`meta-disagreement`) owned by the dedicated `aud_meta_disagreement_split` leaf.

After this leaf:

- Two new selector entries land in the module-scope `STYLESHEET` at [`apps/audience/src/graph/GraphView.tsx:171-236`](../../../apps/audience/src/graph/GraphView.tsx#L171) — `node[rollupStatus = 'disputed']` (overriding `border-color: '#e11d48'` (rose-600) + `border-width: 3`) and `edge[rollupStatus = 'disputed']` (overriding `line-color: '#e11d48'` + `target-arrow-color: '#e11d48'`). Each entry overrides only the properties that differentiate the disputed state from the baseline; typography, geometry (200×80 box), label, shape, background, and text-background fields all inherit from the baseline `node` / `edge` selectors via Cytoscape's per-selector composition.
- Four new Vitest cases land in `apps/audience/src/graph/GraphView.test.tsx` (currently 30 cases after `aud_agreed_styling_mount_assertions` shipped the (cc, dd) mount-time pair) — two structural assertions that the new selector entries appear in `STYLESHEET` with the right style fields, and two mount-time assertions that a node / edge whose projection emitted `data.rollupStatus === 'disputed'` carries the disputed-state computed style after one render tick. Mount-time cases land inline here (not deferred to a follow-up task as the `aud_agreed_styling_mount_assertions` pattern would suggest) because the projection-time emission is already in place — Vitest can resolve the computed style end-to-end on first commit.

## Why it needs to be done

The `m_audience_mvp` milestone in [`tasks/99-milestones.tji`](../../99-milestones.tji) names the entire `aud_graph_rendering` group transitively. The audience surface's reason to exist is broadcast-output to OBS; on a broadcast surface the **disputed-state visual is the load-bearing "viewer, this is the live tension" signal**. Without it, the audience canvas can show "everyone agrees" (slate-700 agreed-state) and "we're still gathering votes" (dashed-faded proposed-state) but cannot show "this is where the actual disagreement is" — the most informative signal for a viewer trying to understand the conversation's live state. With proposed + agreed + disputed all on the canvas, the viewer can scan the graph and immediately read where the conversation is settled, where it is still in motion, and where the moderator is about to surface a resolution choice.

Concretely:

- **`aud_meta_disagreement_split`** (1d, [`tasks/50-audience-and-broadcast.tji:157-161`](../../50-audience-and-broadcast.tji#L157)) extends the same `STYLESHEET` with the meta-disagreement state (the priority level above disputed). That leaf depends on the same `data.rollupStatus` projection field this leaf consumes; once it lands, the four agreement-layer states (proposed / agreed / disputed / meta-disagreement) cover the full agreement-layer space documented in ADR 0005 L19.
- **`aud_per_facet_visualization`** (2d, [`tasks/50-audience-and-broadcast.tji:172-176`](../../50-audience-and-broadcast.tji#L172)) declares `depends !aud_proposed_styling, !aud_agreed_styling, !aud_disputed_styling`. This leaf is the last of the three baseline-state predecessors; landing it unblocks the per-facet visualization task. The card-level rollup this leaf colors is the conservative default; per-facet visualization reads the same `data.facetStatuses` field and subdivides cards to show per-facet sub-states.
- **`aud_withdrawal_animation`** (1d, [`tasks/50-audience-and-broadcast.tji:222-225`](../../50-audience-and-broadcast.tji#L222)) animates the agreed → disputed transition (a withdrawal moves an agreed facet back into the disputed state). Without this leaf's static disputed visual, the animation has no destination state to tween into.
- **The per-state extension pattern reaches the "three siblings" trigger.** [`aud_clean_typography.md`](aud_clean_typography.md) Decision §4 named the `aud_stylesheet_module_extraction` named-future-task (~0.25d) that fires once three sibling tasks have layered per-state selectors on `GraphView.tsx`. With this leaf, the third per-state sibling lands (proposed + agreed + disputed). The closer registers the extraction task per Decision §6 below. Similarly, [`aud_agreed_styling.md`](aud_agreed_styling.md) Decision §3 named `aud_stylesheet_state_color_extraction` (~0.25d, the constants-set extraction; separate from the file-level extraction); this leaf is also that trigger. Both extractions are deferred to named-future tasks rather than landed inline (Decision §6).
- **`aud_visual_regression`** (2d, [`tasks/50-audience-and-broadcast.tji:340-380`](../../50-audience-and-broadcast.tji#L340)) is the pixel-comparison task that already inherits typography + layout + agreed-state + proposed-state pixel-stability deferrals; disputed-state pixel-stability deferred here (Decision §5) follows the same routing.

Architecturally, this leaf mirrors the moderator's `mod_disputed_state_styling` (shipped 2026-05-11, see [`tasks/refinements/moderator-ui/mod_disputed_state_styling.md`](../moderator-ui/mod_disputed_state_styling.md) Status block) but renders into a Cytoscape stylesheet rather than Tailwind classes on a ReactFlow custom node. The moderator stacks two visual signals on nodes — `border-solid border-rose-600` (the solid red border) + `ring-2 ring-rose-500` (the rose halo) — to mark "this needs your attention" at glance distance across a dense canvas. The audience adopts the same color (`#e11d48` rose-600) and substitutes a `border-width: 3` bump for the ring (Cytoscape paints to canvas and has no DOM-ring equivalent; Decision §2 below documents the alternatives weighed). The cross-surface palette match means broadcast composites (audience canvas + future picture-in-picture moderator view) read as one show: red = unresolved, slate-700 = settled, slate-300-dashed = in flight.

## Inputs / context

### ADRs

- [ADR 0004 — Graph libraries: ReactFlow + Cytoscape.js](../../../docs/adr/0004-graph-libraries-reactflow-and-cytoscape.md) — the audience uses Cytoscape's stylesheet for all rendering; per-state visuals must be expressed as selector entries in `STYLESHEET`, not as CSS classes / inline styles on a DOM ancestor (Cytoscape paints to canvas with its own per-selector resolution; CSS cascade does not cross the canvas boundary). Cytoscape has no `ring` / `box-shadow` / DOM-overlay primitive; the per-element visual axes are color + border-width + border-style + opacity + shape + size + overlay-color/opacity/padding.

### §2 — Visual treatment: rose-600 (`#e11d48`) border / line, 3px border-width on nodes only, no fill / shape / opacity change

The baseline `node` selector at [`apps/audience/src/graph/GraphView.tsx:172-191`](../../../apps/audience/src/graph/GraphView.tsx#L172) ships `border-color: #cbd5e1` (slate-300, 1px width) and `background-color: #ffffff`; the agreed-state selector overrides `border-color` to `#334155` (slate-700, still 1px); the proposed-state selector overrides `border-style: 'dashed'` + `opacity: 0.6` (still 1px). The disputed-state node selector overrides `border-color` to `#e11d48` (rose-600) AND `border-width` to `3` (a 2px bump over baseline) — the first per-state branch to override `border-width`.

The baseline `edge` selector at [`apps/audience/src/graph/GraphView.tsx:192-208`](../../../apps/audience/src/graph/GraphView.tsx#L192) ships `line-color: #94a3b8` (slate-400) and `target-arrow-color: #94a3b8`; the disputed-state edge selector overrides both to `#e11d48` (rose-600). No edge-width bump — the edge's 1px default carries the red color at sufficient prominence on the broadcast surface; the directional arrow already draws attention.

Five design axes drove this choice:

1. **Cross-surface consistency.** The moderator's [`mod_disputed_state_styling.md`](../moderator-ui/mod_disputed_state_styling.md) Decisions picked `border-rose-600` (= `#e11d48`) for the node border and `stroke: '#e11d48'` for the edge. Using the same hex in the audience's Cytoscape stylesheet means broadcast composites read as one show — the moderator's pane and the audience canvas use the same red for the same "needs resolution" semantic. The same rationale that pinned slate-700-for-agreed in `aud_agreed_styling.md` Decision §2 applies here.
2. **`border-width: 3` as the Cytoscape analogue of the moderator's ring.** The moderator deliberately stacks two visual signals on disputed nodes — solid red border (`border-rose-600`) AND a rose-500 halo (`ring-2 ring-rose-500`). The ring exists because rose-600 against slate at small-card density on the moderator's dense pane needs the extra visual weight to scan at glance distance. Cytoscape paints to canvas and has no DOM-ring primitive — `box-shadow` does not apply, `outline` does not apply, and the closest functional analogue is either (i) bumping `border-width`, (ii) using Cytoscape's `overlay-color` / `overlay-opacity` / `overlay-padding` primitives, or (iii) accepting color-only and letting the audience-surface's lower density carry the signal. Option (i) is chosen — the 1px → 3px bump produces a visually heavier red boundary that mirrors the moderator's two-signal intent in a single field, without introducing overlay's per-element painting cost or its visual interaction with future per-facet overlay decoration (the `aud_axiom_mark_decoration` task may need overlay for its own decoration, and reserving the overlay axis for that task keeps the rendering pipeline cleaner). The agreed-state's "no width bump" rule (`aud_agreed_styling.md` Decision §2) was state-specific — the agreed state is a "settled, restrained" visual; the disputed state is a "needs attention" visual; the rules legitimately diverge along the priority gradient.
3. **`border-width: 3` is tolerable inside the 200×80 envelope.** The baseline node has 200px width with `text-max-width: 180px` (per `aud_clean_typography.md` Decision §3 / the layout envelope), so effective text room sits roughly at `180 - 2*0 = 180px` baseline and `180 - 2*2 = 176px` at 3px border (the inner border edge moves inward by 2px each side over the 1px baseline). That is a ~2.2% effective text-room loss — below the legibility threshold (`aud_clean_typography` confirmed legibility at 14px / 600 weight inside 180px text-max-width on the 1080p OBS baseline; a 2.2% effective room loss is well within the safety margin and does not regress legibility). At 1440p the relative loss is identical; the absolute loss is 2px per side regardless of resolution because the stroke is canvas-rendered, not CSS-scaled.
4. **Edges: color-only at 1px, no width bump.** The moderator's edge styling is color-only (`stroke: '#e11d48'`, no `strokeWidth` override). The audience follows the same posture — edges already carry directional / role-label weight; adding a width bump to disputed edges would visually overpower the surrounding agreed / proposed edges and crowd the canvas. The color alone carries the signal.
5. **No background-color fill, no opacity change, no shape change.** Alternative — pre-tint the node fill (`#fef2f2` rose-50, or similar). Rejected because (a) breaks the agreed-state's no-fill posture without strong functional gain — the red border at 3px already carries the signal, (b) crosses into the "faded" visual axis the proposed-state owns (and an opacity-keyed comparison between proposed-faded-white and disputed-tinted-rose would become harder to read against compressed video), (c) introduces a new visual axis (fill) that future state-styling tasks would have to either match or explain. Color + width is sufficient.

Alternatives surfaced and rejected:

- **Overlay halo (`overlay-color: '#f43f5e'`, `overlay-opacity: 0.3`, `overlay-padding: 4`).** The closest functional mirror of the moderator's `ring-rose-500`. Rejected because (a) it introduces a new rendering axis that competes with future axiom-mark / annotation overlays (which `aud_axiom_mark_decoration` and `aud_annotation_rendering` may need to claim), (b) overlay rendering interacts with Cytoscape's z-stacking in ways that are version-sensitive (Cytoscape 3.x has shifted overlay-paint semantics across point releases), and (c) the border-width bump achieves visually equivalent attention-grabbing weight with a single field that is well-understood and stable across Cytoscape versions. The simpler approach wins absent a concrete need the simpler approach can't satisfy.
- **Color-only at 1px (`border-color: '#e11d48'`, keep `border-width: 1`).** Rejected because (a) on the broadcast surface at 1080p / 1440p, a 1px red border against a 1px slate border is visually subtle — the color is the only differentiator, and red can read as "any other dark color" against compressed video unless given visual weight, (b) the moderator deliberately stacked two signals for disputed precisely because color alone wasn't enough; replicating the under-weighted single-signal version on the audience would underperform the cross-surface contract.
- **Different red (`crimson #dc143c`, `red-700 #b91c1c`, etc.).** Rejected — cross-surface palette match with the moderator is the stronger force; rose-600 is the moderator's pinned value, and the audience matches.

No new ADR is required. The visual specification is already in ADR 0005 L19 ("disputed marker"); the moderator's task already settled the specific hex; this leaf adopts the hex and translates the ring → width-bump for the Cytoscape rendering tech.
